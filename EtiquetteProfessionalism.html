Etiquette Professionalism PDF Summary

Socialization and Expectations:

• Socialization involves acquiring knowledge, language, and values to fit into different groups or roles.

• Adapting to new roles and social expectations is a continuous process in life.

Etiquette as a Behavioral Script:

• Etiquette is a behavioral script, a set of expectations for social behavior within a society or group.

Personal Image and Professionalism:

• Your personal image is akin to your brand; it influences perceptions in the workplace.

• Professional grooming reflects how others perceive you in a professional setting.

First Impressions and Behavior:

• The lecture emphasizes the importance of first impressions in various aspects of life.

• Non-verbal cues, such as posture, gestures, and eye contact, play a significant role in social interactions.

What I learned

Etiquette and Social Acceptance:

• Understanding etiquette and social norms is crucial for social acceptance.

• Non-verbal behavior, like handshakes and posture, can impact how others perceive us.

Dining and Social Etiquette:

• Detailed dining etiquette guidelines were provided, emphasizing the importance of manners in social settings.

• Behavior at cocktail parties and when opening doors were also highlighted.

Personal Image and Grooming:

• The lecture emphasized the significance of personal grooming and how it influences perceptions of professionalism.

How, I can apply them

Practicing Etiquette:

• Apply etiquette rules in social and professional settings to make favorable impressions.

• Focus on non-verbal cues, grooming, and social behavior to convey professionalism and confidence.

Improving Social Interactions:

• Implement dining etiquette guidelines during social events to make positive impressions.

Enhancing Personal Image:

• Use grooming habits to reflect professionalism and attention to detail in the workplace.
